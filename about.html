Our Origin

Since 2019

Onebody opened its doors in Yaba, Lagos because a group of young Christians passionate about gospel-centered music and charity wanted to grow deeper in their knowledge of Jesus.

Founded by three friends — Taiwo Oyemade, Kehinde Oyemade, and Olarewaju Omomeji — they met at home every Friday for three years before formally opening, writing music and hosting retreats together.

What Shapes Us

Core convictions.

These convictions give visitors theological and cultural clarity before they ever step into a service.

Jesus-centered teaching +
We want the message and teachings of Jesus Christ to remain central in the life of the church, not peripheral.
Music and worship as witness +
Music is not a side identity for us. It has always been one of the ways the Lord has used Onebody to preach Christ and gather people.
Discipleship with direction +
SCAD and LOVA exist because we believe disciples should be sought, converted, assimilated, and deployed with clear intention.
Love made practical +
Gospel renewal should be visible in generosity, local charity, service, and the way we treat people in our city.

Renewed Discipleship

SCAD

"Therefore go and make disciples of all nations…" burns in our hearts. We have refined our discipleship model around four stages.

S

Seeking

Through music, visual content, affinity groups, and special events, we always seek new souls.

C

Converting

In our weekly gatherings and home meetings, we create real opportunities for conversions.

A

Assimilating

An 8-module, 16-week discipleship program — The Love Vision Academy — assimilates new members.

D

Deploying

Every disciple goes out and seeks new souls through the grace and gifts of the Holy Spirit.
