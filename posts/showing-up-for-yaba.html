Why We Keep Showing Up for Yaba

Charity is not strategy. It is obedience. A reflection on why our work in Yaba is inseparable from our worship.

We do not serve Yaba because it makes us look good. We serve Yaba because we are in Yaba, and this is our neighbourhood, and these are our people. That is the whole reason.

There is a version of church charity that is essentially a PR exercise — showing up seasonally, taking photographs, leaving. We do not want to be that. We want to be the kind of community that is present in the mundane, unglamorous, long-haul way that love actually requires.

What we have learned

After several years of community work in Yaba, we have learned a few things. First, the people we serve almost always teach us more than we teach them. Second, consistency matters more than scale. Showing up reliably with less is more meaningful than arriving dramatically with more.

Third — and this is the one that surprised us most — service changes the server. Something happens to a congregation that regularly puts on an apron and gets to work for their neighbours. It makes Sunday feel different. It makes the songs mean more.

Worship and works

James was not writing poetry when he said faith without works is dead. He was making an anatomical observation. A body that does not move is not resting — it is dead.

Our charity work is not a separate department from our church. It is the same church, expressing the same love, in a different room. We keep showing up for Yaba because we cannot worship the God who became flesh and stay entirely in our heads.
