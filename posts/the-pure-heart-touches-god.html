The Pure Heart Touches God

By Jennifer Asomie

An article adapted from a sermon by Pastor Taiwo Oyemade titled "God is Speaking! Can you Hear! (part 2)" at Onebody Church, Yaba Lagos on Sunday, 10th March 2024.

Throughout history, humanity has struggled with the mystery of divine communication. Many have sought to confine God's voice to certain channels, relegating His presence to specific individuals or places. However, as scripture reveals, God's voice cannot be limited by human constraints; He can speak through anyone or anything (as in the Burning Bush or Balaam's Donkey). This truth, rooted in the omnipotent nature of God, is exemplified in various biblical passages, with dreams serving as a major mode of divine communication.

The Gospel of Matthew, in chapters 5, verses 3 to 10, presents a profound insight into the reciprocating nature of divine interaction. These verses are called The Beatitudes which describe supreme blessedness. A noteworthy truth from these verses is that everyone who attains "blessed" cannot be labelled as "cursed."

These Beatitudes can be likened to medals adorning the believers' journey. Each beatitude represents a distinct facet of righteousness, honoring those who embody these virtues. This metaphorical understanding highlights the multifaceted nature of spiritual growth and attestation.

Jesus articulates the principle of mercy in one of these beatitudes, proclaiming:

"Blessed are the merciful, for they will be shown mercy."

Matthew 5:7 NIV

This divine reciprocity underscores the interconnectedness of human actions and divine favor. It shows the divine empowerment bestowed upon individuals, enabling them to live righteously. It is important to note in the discourse on mercy that it extends beyond mere acts of kindness. It also encompasses fidelity and commitment, such as faithfulness to one's spouse for example.

More relevant to our conversation about communication is a beatitude in Matthew 5:8:

"Blessed are the pure in heart, for they will see God."

Matthew 5:8 NIV

The central focus of this proclamation is the condition of the heart. The heart, also known as the "cardia," serves here not merely as a blood-pumping organ but as the effective center of our being. The psalmist, in Psalm 24:3-4, delineates the conditions for dwelling in God's presence, emphasizing the necessity of purity and righteousness:

"Who may ascend the mountain of the Lord? Who may stand in his holy place? The one who has clean hands and a pure heart, who does not trust in an idol or swear by a false god."

Psalms 24:3–4 NIV

Proverbs 4:23 admonishes believers to guard their hearts diligently, as it determines the trajectory of their lives. The state of the heart, reflective of one's inner desires and intentions, significantly influences the quality of communication with God.

In exploring the connection between the heart and hearing from God, Matthew 23:25-28 and Matthew 15:15-20 provide valuable insights. These passages highlight the importance of purity of heart, showing the detrimental effects of internal impurity on external conduct. Impurity arises when the cycle of impure thoughts culminates, manifesting in actions and speech.

To prevent this contamination, believers must exercise vigilance in guarding their hearts against corrupt influences. The biggest challenge in hearing from God lies in the condition of our hearts. Satan, recognizing this vulnerability, often tempts believers with grey areas, enticing them away from the clear and narrow path of righteousness. Romans 12:1-2 exhorts believers to renew their minds, resisting conformity to the world's standards. Similarly, Habakkuk 2:1 urges believers to remain vigilant, and attentive to the promptings of the divine.

In conclusion, the journey of divine communication transcends human limitations, encompassing the multifaceted dynamics of the heart and righteousness. By embracing the transformative power of mercy and purity, believers can attune their hearts to the voice of God, navigating through the complexities of spiritual discernment. As custodians of the heart, believers are called to safeguard against the encroachment of impurity vigilantly, remaining steadfast in their pursuit of divine communion.
