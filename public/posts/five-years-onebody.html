Five Years of Onebody: A Look Back

From three friends writing songs in a sitting room to a church family of hundreds — here is the story so far.

In 2016, Taiwo, Kehinde, and Olarewaju started meeting on Friday evenings. There was no vision document. There was a guitar, a Bible, and a shared conviction that they wanted to know Jesus more than they wanted anything else.

For three years, that sitting room was their church. They wrote songs. They prayed. They wrestled with scripture. People started showing up. The room got fuller.

2019: Opening the doors

Onebody formally opened in Yaba in 2019. The first Sunday was smaller than they had hoped and larger than they had expected — which is probably how it should be. They did not know what they were starting. They just knew they were supposed to start.

The early years were a lesson in letting go of expectations. The church that grew was not exactly the church they had planned. It was better in some ways, harder in others, and consistently surprising.

What five years has taught us

Five years in, here is what we know: the church is more resilient than we imagined and more fragile than we assumed. Community is possible — genuinely, beautifully possible — but it requires more honesty and more forgiveness than feels comfortable.

We have buried people we loved. We have celebrated marriages and children we did not expect. We have watched people walk away and watched people come home. All of it is the church. All of it belongs to the story.

We are grateful. For Yaba. For the people who showed up. For the God who, somehow, keeps showing up too.
