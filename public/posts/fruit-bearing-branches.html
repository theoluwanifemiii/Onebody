Fruit Bearing Branches

By Jennifer Asomie

An article adapted from a sermon by Pastor Pelumi Ajayi titled "Fruit Bearing Branches (Part 1)" at Onebody Church, Yaba Lagos on Sunday, 24th March 2024.

"I am the true grapevine, and my Father is the gardener. He cuts off every branch of mine that doesn't produce fruit, and he prunes the branches that do bear fruit so they will produce even more. You have already been pruned and purified by the message I have given you. Remain in me, and I will remain in you. For a branch cannot produce fruit if it is severed from the vine, and you cannot be fruitful unless you remain in me."

John 15:1–4 NLT

In the Gospel of John, chapter 15, verses 1-18, Jesus employs the metaphor of a vine and its branches to discuss truths about the relationship between himself, his disciples, and their mission to the world. John 15:1 introduces Jesus as the true grapevine — the genuine reality. This assertion is buttressed in John 6:32-34, where Jesus distinguishes himself as the true bread from heaven, surpassing any previous manifestations or copies.

Central to Jesus' discourse is the metaphor of pruning in John 15 verses 2-3. This illustrates the process of purification and refinement that believers undergo to bear fruit. Here, the Word of God serves as the pruning tool. This is affirmed in John 17:17 which emphasizes the Word's transformative power in shaping disciples into the image of Christ.

The use of the term pruning carries strong implications. It straightaway entails discomfort and sacrifice. But it is essential for ensuring fruitfulness. This truth echoes throughout the scripture, portraying God as the faithful gardener who tends to his vineyard with care and wisdom. However, the response to this divine cultivation varies among individuals, leading to either acceptance or resistance. Furthermore, bearing fruit is contingent upon abiding in Christ, as shown in verse 4. This reciprocal relationship underscores the inseparable union between the believer and Christ, facilitated by the indwelling of the Word.

"Yes, I am the vine; you are the branches. Those who remain in me, and I in them, will produce much fruit. For apart from me you can do nothing."

John 15:5 NLT

As reiterated in John 15:5, apart from Christ, human efforts are futile, highlighting the necessity of complete reliance on him. The mandate to bear fruit extends beyond personal transformation to encompass the mission of disciple-making. True disciples, as depicted in John 4:35-36, actively participate in bringing others into the fold of eternal life, echoing the call in Luke 5:10.

John 15:6 further elucidates this by highlighting the indispensable role of abiding in Christ and his teachings, as depicted in John 8:31. This commission, rooted in Matthew 28:20, shows the imperative of fidelity to Christ's teachings in spreading the gospel.

In conclusion, the essence of discipleship lies in abiding in Christ and faithfully adhering to his Word, as elucidated in Matthew 11:3-6. The branches' sole responsibility is to remain connected to the true vine, drawing sustenance and strength. By abiding in Christ and embracing the transformative power of the Word, believers become conduits of divine grace, bearing fruit that glorifies God and perpetuates his kingdom on earth.
