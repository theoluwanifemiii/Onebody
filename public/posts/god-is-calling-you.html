God Is Calling You

By Jennifer Asomie

An article adapted from a sermon by Pastor Taiwo Oyemade titled "God is Calling You (Part 2)" at Onebody Church, Yaba Lagos on Sunday, 17th March 2024.

"So the Lord called a third time, and once more Samuel got up and went to Eli. 'Here I am. Did you call me?' Then Eli realized it was the Lord who was calling the boy. So he said to Samuel, 'Go and lie down again, and if someone calls again, say, "Speak, Lord, your servant is listening."' So Samuel went back to bed."

1 Samuel 3:8–9 NLT

1 Samuel 3:8-9 marks a pivotal moment in the life of Samuel, a young boy who would become one of Israel's greatest judges. It gives insight into the nature of hearing and responding to God's voice, as well as the responsibilities incumbent upon those who mentor the next generation of spiritual leaders.

Initially, when Samuel hears a voice calling him in the night, he instinctively seeks guidance from Eli. Samuel's response reflects a common human tendency — to rely on familiar voices or sources of authority when encountering the unfamiliar. Eli, recognizing the divine nature of Samuel's experience, instructs him to respond to the call saying, "Speak, Lord, for your servant is listening." This exchange highlights a crucial distinction between merely hearing and truly listening; while many may hear the voice of God, not all are attentive or responsive.

Moreover, Eli's role in guiding Samuel serves as a reminder of our duty as elders and leaders to nurture and mentor the younger ones in our community. Just as Eli instructed Samuel, we are also called to teach and equip our people to recognize and respond to God's voice. This involves instilling in them the understanding that God mostly initiates communication with them and that they must be prepared to heed His call when He reaches out.

The subsequent revelation that Samuel receives — a message of judgment against Eli's household — illustrates the weightiness of divine communication. It shows that God's initial words to His prophets may entail difficult truths or warnings. This theme resonates with other biblical accounts, such as the prophet Jeremiah's call, where God commissions him to confront his people with messages of judgment and repentance (Jeremiah 1:7-10).

The narrative in Luke 5:1-11 offers additional insights into responding to God's call. In this passage, Jesus instructs Simon Peter to launch his boat into the deep water and let down his nets for a catch. Initially hesitant due to a night of fruitless fishing, Peter nonetheless obeys Jesus' command. The subsequent miraculous catch of fish serves as a testament to the power of faith and obedience.

The story of Peter's obedience shows how God's will precedes our needs. Like Peter, we may sometimes doubt or question God's instructions, particularly when they seem illogical or contrary to our expectations. However, as Matthew 6:33 reminds us, seeking first God's kingdom and righteousness aligns our priorities with His divine purposes.

Luke 5:5 highlights the role of faith in responding to God's word, even when it defies human understanding. Peter's willingness to trust Jesus' command, despite his doubts and frustrations, demonstrates faith rooted in divine authority rather than logical reasoning.

Furthermore, Luke 6:38 emphasizes the principle of giving and receiving, suggesting that generosity leads to abundance. This is exemplified in the story of Hannah, who selflessly dedicates her son Samuel to God's service and is subsequently blessed with additional children (1 Samuel 1:27-28).

In conclusion, the passages referenced help buttress the importance of attentive listening, faithful obedience, and trust in God's will. As we heed His call and push our boats deeper into the waters of faith, we position ourselves for divine breakthroughs and miraculous manifestations of His power.
