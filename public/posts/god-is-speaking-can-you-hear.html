God Is Speaking! Can You Hear?

By Jennifer Asomie

An article adapted from a sermon by Pastor Taiwo Oyemade titled "God is Speaking, Can You Hear?" at Onebody Church, Yaba Lagos on Wednesday, 6th March 2024.

Hearing God's voice begins with a fundamental prerequisite: open ears. This is underscored throughout the scriptures, highlighting the importance of reception and attentiveness to divine communication.

Proverbs 20:12 declares, "The hearing ear and the seeing eye, the Lord has made them both." This verse highlights that God has granted us the ability to hear and perceive, but it is our responsibility to cultivate an openness to His voice. Hearing God is also not for the lazy-hearted as supported by Proverbs 18:15 which states, "An intelligent heart acquires knowledge, and the ear of the wise seeks knowledge." This warning suggests that hearing God requires diligence and active pursuit, echoing the sentiment that "spiritual laziness" impedes divine communication.

Central to the discussion is the recognition of the incomparable value of God's voice. Matthew 4:4 records Jesus' words:

"Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word that comes from the mouth of God."

This statement elevates God's voice as the most significant treasure, emphasising its vital role in sustaining spiritual life.

The story of Joseph is an example of the power of hearing God. Genesis 41:16 recounts Joseph's acknowledgement that the interpretation of dreams comes from God, proving his ability to hear and understand divine messages. Similarly, Daniel's encounter with Nebuchadnezzar in Daniel 2 demonstrates how hearing God enables the resolution of complex problems, as seen in Daniel's interpretation of the king's dream (Daniel 2:18).

The profound encounters of prophets like Ezekiel serve as vivid illustrations of the intimate communication between God and His servants. Ezekiel 2:1-2 depicts Ezekiel's commissioning by God, highlighting the depth of their relational dialogue. These interactions highlight the conversational nature of divine communication, inviting believers into a similar depth of relationship with God.

Moreover, Zechariah 4:6 reinforces the notion that hearing God is facilitated by His provision and empowerment. This verse assures believers that it is not by their own might but by the Spirit of God that they can stand and hear His voice. The scriptures also emphasise the urgency of heeding God's voice.

"Long ago, at many times and in many ways, God spoke to our fathers by the prophets, but in these last days, he has spoken to us by his Son."

Hebrews 1:1–2

This passage underscores the significance of listening to the voice of Jesus Christ in the present age. Driving home this truth: the ability to hear God's voice is contingent upon faith and obedience to Christ. John 3:3, 5-11 makes it even clearer that only those who believe and follow Christ can discern God's voice and warns us against the influence of deceptive spirits.

Despite potential challenges or uncertainties, believers are urged to trust and follow God's guidance unreservedly. That is why John 10:5, 27 tells of the obedient response of Christ's sheep to His voice, highlighting the necessity of faithfulness and receptivity.

In conclusion, the Word provides a comprehensive framework for understanding and pursuing divine communication; from the foundational requirement of open ears to imperative obedience, the journey of hearing God's voice is illuminated as a transformative and indispensable aspect of the believer's walk with God.
