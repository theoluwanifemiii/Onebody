What LOVA Is Teaching Us About Growth

The Onebody Academy is not a Bible college. It is a formation process — and it is changing how we love.

LOVA — the Lovers of God Academy — started from a simple observation: information about the Bible does not automatically produce transformation by it. People can know a lot of theology and still be unkind. They can pass every doctrinal test and fail the love test.

We wanted something different. Not a curriculum to be completed, but a process to be inhabited. LOVA was our attempt to build that.

What formation actually looks like

LOVA runs across four stages: Salvation, Community, Action, and Discipleship — SCAD. Each stage is designed to move a person deeper into a particular posture, not just a particular knowledge set.

The Community stage, for example, is not a course on ecclesiology. It is a lived experience of being in genuine relationship with people you would not have chosen. The Action stage is not a lecture on missiology. It is actual service, in Yaba, with your hands.

What it is teaching us

The people who go through LOVA come out different. Not perfectly — there is no programme for that. But differently oriented. More patient with people. More aware of their own blind spots. More willing to be wrong.

And it is teaching us something as a church: growth that does not show up in how people treat each other is not the growth we are after. LOVA is helping us measure what actually matters.
