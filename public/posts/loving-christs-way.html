Loving Christ's Way

By Mary Oiza Egbe

There's one word in today's world that suffers from overuse and yet in its usage, very little of it is propagated. It is the word "Love." We see the templates created around love and wonder just how much about love we really comprehend. Is this how love was meant to be expressed or felt? Is this what love is all about? Fighting for our own selfish desires and calling it self-love; bringing down another to raise our own just because we care only for those around us; and/or wishing the downfall of another so you can have your way?

Before we get any deeper into this discussion, what does the Bible say about love?

"1 If I could speak all the languages of earth and of angels, but didn't love others, I would only be a noisy gong or a clanging cymbal. 2 If I had the gift of prophecy, and if I understood all of God's secret plans and possessed all knowledge, and if I had such faith that I could move mountains, but didn't love others, I would be nothing. 3 If I gave everything I have to the poor and even sacrificed my body, I could boast about it; but if I didn't love others, I would have gained nothing. 4 Love is patient and kind. Love is not jealous or boastful or proud 5 or rude. It does not demand its own way. It is not irritable, and it keeps no record of being wronged. 6 It does not rejoice about injustice but rejoices whenever the truth wins out. 7 Love never gives up, never loses faith, is always hopeful, and endures through every circumstance. 8 Prophecy and speaking in unknown languages and special knowledge will become useless. But love will last forever!"

1 Corinthians 13:1–8

If we compare what the Bible says about love with what we see today, there is a huge contrast and it almost looks as if we cannot love according to the template seen in the Bible. This is how God wants us to love. He showed us the greatest form of love by sending His only son to die on the cross for you and me (John 3:16). It makes no sense right? I mean why should anyone let go of their only child for a people who wouldn't even comprehend the extent of the sacrifice let alone be willing to do the same for others. Yet still do it anyway? I'm personally perplexed at such a show of love.

How did Christ love?

At the heart of Christ's teachings is the commandment to love one another as He loved us. This love is not conditional but encompasses all. It's a love that extends even to one's enemies, challenging individuals to confront their biases and prejudices with compassion and understanding.

Jesus Christ loved differently; He sought to bring you and me into God's purpose by first appearing in the flesh and was offered up as a sacrifice so that we can become children of God. Jesus throughout His walk on earth, did only that which pleased the Father, even till the point of death. Let's have a look at this verse from the Bible, John 14:31, Jesus said:

"…but I will do what the Father requires of me, so that the world will know that I love the Father. Come, let's be going."

Jesus loved the Father by doing what the Father required of him. He loved us by paying the ultimate price of dying on the cross for us so that we too can boldly approach the Father. In loving God, what is required of us is strict obedience to what He requires us to do. Jesus said:

"If you love me, obey my commandments."

John 14:15 NLT

Using Christ as a template, if we must love as He did, we need to lay aside our selfish interests, and put God first and the brethren next. Jesus Christ showed this path (Ephesians 5:2) when He laid down His life for our sake. He saw the joy before Him and endured the cross. This love does not seek its own, it seeks to satisfy the interest of others above personal desires and ambition. In the same manner, in which Christ showed his love towards us through the cross, we ought to love God's people in that way.

"17 If someone has enough money to live well and sees a brother or sister in need but shows no compassion — how can God's love be in that person? 18 Dear children, let's not merely say that we love each other; let us show the truth by our actions. 19 Our actions will show that we belong to the truth, so we will be confident when we stand before God."

1 John 3:17–19

Loving Christ's way is not always easy. It requires a willingness to step outside of comfort zones and to extend grace to those who may not deserve it. Yet, it is through this transformative love that individuals and communities can experience what genuine love is.

As followers of Christ, let us strive to embody His love in all that we do — to walk in compassion, extend forgiveness, and serve others with humility. In doing so, we participate in the ongoing work of building a world marked by love — a world that reflects the transformative power of Christ's love for all humanity.
