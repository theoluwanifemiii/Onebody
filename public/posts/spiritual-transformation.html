Spiritual Transformation

By Jennifer Asomie

An article adapted from a sermon by Pastor Taiwo Oyemade titled "God is Calling You" at Onebody Church, Yaba Lagos on Wednesday, 13th March 2024.

In the spirit, transformation is not merely a superficial alteration of behavior or speech; it demands a profound change in the core of one's being. Romans 12:1-2 underscores the necessity of a spiritual metamorphosis, urging believers to renew their minds, aligning them with the divine will.

"And so, dear brothers and sisters, I plead with you to give your bodies to God because of all he has done for you. Let them be a living and holy sacrifice — the kind he will find acceptable. This is truly the way to worship him. Don't copy the behavior and customs of this world, but let God transform you into a new person by changing the way you think. Then you will learn to know God's will for you, which is good and pleasing and perfect."

Romans 12:1–2 NLT

Central to this transformation is the purity of the heart. The purity of one's heart is deemed one of the greatest challenges within the church. As highlighted in John 17:17, God's word serves as the instrument for this sanctification, purification, and cleansing of believers.

Reflecting on 1 Samuel 3:1:

"Meanwhile, the boy Samuel served the Lord by assisting Eli. Now in those days, messages from the Lord were very rare, and visions were quite uncommon."

1 Samuel 3:1 NLT

We read about a period of spiritual silence, where the voice of God was scarce. However, this silence did not denote inactivity; rather, it illustrated God's mysterious workings beyond human comprehension. Even in moments of apparent silence, divine destinies unfold, as seen in the life of Hannah, and Samuel her little son.

The story of the early days of Samuel unveils the significance of consecration to the Lord. Samuel's uniqueness stemmed from his dedication to God, symbolized by his being "given over" to the divine purpose. This separation unto the Lord, as emphasized in 2 Timothy 2:20-21, is a hallmark of those destined for significant roles in God's plan.

1 Samuel 3:2-7 provides insights into discerning the voice of God — a vital aspect of spiritual growth. Hearing and knowing the voice of God requires intimacy with Him, as articulated in John 8:31 and John 15:15. Yet, the scarcity of spiritual mentors like Eli who will point out God's voice to young Samuels impedes this process.

Dedication and faithfulness to God, exemplified by figures like Joseph, are essential attributes for those seeking to fulfil their divine calling. The scarcity of individuals akin to Hannah in the contemporary church highlights a profound challenge — the reluctance to relinquish prized possessions, even when they are meant for greater purposes.

In conclusion, the journey of spiritual transformation demands more than surface-level changes — it necessitates a profound shift in mindset, purity of heart, and unwavering dedication to God's will. Through scripture and reflection, believers can navigate the challenges of the spiritual realm, equipping themselves to discern divine guidance amidst the cacophony of worldly distractions.
